Storm Calculator

Solve for CAPE, Shear (Knots), or Angle

Instructions: Enter exactly two of the three inputs below. The script will solve for the missing third parameter. If you fill in all three or only one, it will show an error.

Here, Shear is interpreted as the difference in wind speed (in knots) from the surface to 6 km altitude, and the updraft speed is given by:

w = α × √(2 × CAPE) × D

with α = 0.6 (i.e. 60% realization), and the drag‐factor,

D = 1 - (Cd × ρ(z) × z / k)

where ρ(z) is the air density at storm height and the storm height is approximated as:

z = 4000 + 2√(CAPE) (in meters)

Constants used:

  • Cd = 0.8
  • k = 1e6

If one parameter was missing, it is solved from the other two.
